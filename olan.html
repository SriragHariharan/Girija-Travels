Olan

Kerala Olan is a traditional dish made during Onasadya. It’s usually made with white pumpkin (Ash-Gourd or Kumbalanga), Red Cow Peas and Coconut Milk. This is mild and tasty stew served usually for the Onam Sadya. With the combination of curry leaves, coconut milk, green chili and coconut oil, this is very flavorful and tasty.



Ingredients


  • ½ cup Red Cow Peas
  • 2 cups Ash gourd
  • 4 nos Green Chili
  • 1 cup Thin Coconut Milk
  • ¼ cup Thick Coconut Milk
  • 1 sprig Curry leaves
  • Salt , to taste
  • 1 tablespoon Coconut Oil
  • A cute Smile on Heart

Preparation


  1. To begin Soak the Cow peas in the water overnight. (minimum 6 hours)

  2. Next day, Drain the water and rinse the Karamani again. Drain extra water. Add ½ tsp of salt.

  3. Take this to a pressure cooker, add 1 cup of water and pressure cook over 1 whistle. Since we have soaked the Karamani already, it will get cooked over just 1 whistle

  4. Once the pressure is released, check if the Karamani is cooked and keep it aside.

  5. Remove the skin of the ash gourd (white pumpkin) and cube it into medium size pieces. Keep the chopped vegetable in the water.

  6. add thin coconut milk. Cover the pan, keep the flame in medium and allow the vegetable to get cooked in the coconut milk.

  7. In between, please check if the ash gourd is cooking and add more water / coconut milk

  8. Once the ashgourd is cooked completely, add slit green chili, sprig of curry leaves and salt. Give a good stir.

  9. Now add the thick coconut milk. Keep the olan in low flame for another couple of minutes and give it a good mix. Make sure that we don't bring the mixture to boil

  10. Finally garnish with coconut oil and few more curry leaves.

  11. Relish the delicious Olan with Sambar or Mor Curry and enjoy this delicious Olan !
